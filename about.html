Definitions

Order of the Arrow (Abbreviated as OA)

The National Honor Society of Scouting which members are elected by their fellow scouts.

Ceremonies

Order of the Arrow has many ceremonies including ordeal, brotherhood, and the vigil ceremony. These have scripted parts and are based on Indian traditions.

Your elangomat will walk you through your journey and guide you through your ordeal.

National Order of the Arrow Council (Abbreviated as NOAC)

NOAC is a council of every arrowmen who signs up in the nation, in 2022 having over six-thousand. This event connects all OA members and allows remote delegates.

OA High Adventure

OA high adventure is a great program that all four high adventure bases participate in. It works in which you go for an extended period of time before your trip, and then the second half is the actual camp. OA high adventure can make the costs go to less than half that of the original amount making it affordable to people who originally were unable to go without financial aid. FAQ

What Is The OA?

The Order of the Arrow is Scoutings National Honor Society, drawing scouts from across the nation to serve in cheerful service under the bonds of brotherhood.

When do you age out?

You officially become an adult in the OA at 21 years old, rather than 18 as it is in Scouts BSA.

How often do you meet?

Each Lodge holds meetings at different times, though there is typically an event every month or few months. In order to see what events are available, go to your lodge website.

What is Conclave?

Conclave is a gathering of OA members from across your section, which is a collection of multiple lodges in your area. Most of the time, the site where Conclave occurs varies each time as each lodge gets to host it.

What is NOAC?

NOAC, or National Order of the Arrow Conference, is a gathering of Arrowmen from across the nation and internationally on a college campus for a week, hosting trainings, recreational events, and plenty of opportunities to meet new friends.

What is Brotherhood?

Brotherhood is the second level in the OA, where members seal their commitment to the order. In order to obtain your brotherhood, you must complete your brotherhood trail, available 6 months after your Ordeal.
